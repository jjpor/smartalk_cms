Here are some important terms for discussing company culture, values, and environment.

{# Contenuto Statico (per riferimento/spiegazione) #}
  • Company culture: The shared values, beliefs, and behaviors in a workplace.
  • Mission statement: A company’s main goal and reason for existing.
  • Core values: Important beliefs that guide a company’s decisions and actions.
  • Inclusive: Open to people of all backgrounds and differences.
  • Toxic environment: A workplace full of negativity, fear, or conflict.
  • Team-oriented: Focused on collaboration and working together.
  • Micromanagement: When a manager controls too many small details.
  • Autonomy: The ability to work independently and make decisions.
  • Feedback culture: A workplace where giving and receiving feedback is common.
  • Work-life balance: The ability to manage work and personal life in a healthy way.

These questions are designed to get you thinking and talking about your experiences and preferences in the workplace.

Your Experiences

  • What aspects of company culture make you feel most supported as an employee?
  • Have you ever felt uncomfortable because of a company’s culture? What caused it?
  • What small changes would improve the culture at your current or previous job?
  • How important is it to have friendships at work?
  • Do you feel comfortable giving feedback to your manager? Why or why not?

Your Values & Preferences

  • What does "work-life balance" mean to you? Has a company ever helped you achieve it?
  • Do you prefer working for a company with clear rules or more flexibility? Why?
  • What motivates you more: team goals or individual recognition?
  • Do you feel like your values match your company’s values? Why or why not?
  • If you were starting your own company, what kind of culture would you create?

{% call info_card('exercises', 'Collaborative Exercise: Values & Expectations', initial_state='show') %}

This exercise can be done with a partner or in a small group. Follow the steps below to practice discussing culture and values.

The Scenario

Imagine you are interviewing for a new job. Your goal is to understand the company's culture and find out if their values match yours. The person you are with will be the "hiring manager."

Exercise Steps

Step 1: Set the Stage

The "hiring manager" should describe a common company culture (e.g., fast-paced, very team-oriented, a lot of autonomy, etc.). The "candidate" should use the vocabulary and questions from this guide to ask for clarification and examples to see if the culture is a good fit.

Step 2: The Conversation

Role-play the conversation for 5-7 minutes. The candidate should ask about specific values, work-life balance, and feedback culture, using conditionals to ask hypothetical questions (e.g., "If I were to join the team, would I have autonomy over my projects?").

Step 3: Debrief & Discuss

Switch roles and repeat the exercise. Afterward, discuss these questions together:

  • What questions were most helpful for understanding the culture?
  • Which values were most important to you as the candidate?
  • What did you learn from this role-play that you can apply in a real job interview?
