Motivation is the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way. It's the engine that drives us to achieve goals, whether they're personal, academic, or professional.

Two Main Types:

Intrinsic Motivation: This comes from within. You do something because it's inherently rewarding, interesting, or enjoyable to you. It's about personal satisfaction, not external pressure.
Example: Solving a puzzle because you enjoy the challenge.
Extrinsic Motivation: This is driven by external rewards or consequences. You do something to earn a reward, avoid punishment, or meet an external expectation.
Example: Working overtime to receive a financial bonus.

{% call info_card('idioms', '3. Motivation Idioms', initial_state='show') %}

Here are some common idioms related to drive, energy, and enthusiasm.

{# Idiom 1 #}
Dial it back

To reduce the intensity of something, or tone it down.

"When Lucas started getting all excited, his mother said: Hey Lucas, dial it back a bit!"

{# Idiom 2 #}
Eager beaver

A person who is hardworking and enthusiastic, sometimes considered overzealous.

"The new accountant works all the time - first to arrive and last to leave. He's a real eager beaver!"

{# Idiom 3 #}
Eat, sleep and breathe something

To be so enthusiastic and passionate about something that you think about it constantly.

"He's an enthusiastic golfer; he eats, sleeps and breathes golf!"

{# Idiom 4 #}
(On the) edge of one's seat

Very interested in something, finding it both extremely exciting and nerve-wracking.

"Look at Bob! He's on the edge of his seat watching that rugby match!"

{# Idiom 5 #}
Go the extra mile

To do more than what is expected of you.

"You can count on Tom; he's always willing to go the extra mile."

{# Idiom 6 #}
Hold your horses

To tell someone to slow down and not rush into action.

"Hold your horses! We need to get the customer's approval first!"

{# Idiom 7 #}
Put your heart (and soul) into something

To be very enthusiastic and invest a lot of energy and hard work.

"Paul was determined to make a success of the project. He put his heart and soul into it."

{# Idiom 8 #}
Run out of steam

To lose impetus, energy, or enthusiasm.

"The anti-immigrant movement seems to be running out of steam."
