{% call info_card('vocabulary', '2. Vocabulary: Idioms for Professionalism', initial_state='show') %}

(Teacher explains, students use in sentences or roleplays.)

  • Dress for success - Dress in a way that will help you succeed.
  • Put your best foot forward - Make the best possible impression.
  • Keep your cool - Stay calm in stressful situations.
  • Go the extra mile - Do more than is expected.
  • By the book - Follow the rules exactly.
  • On the ball - Be alert and quick to understand.
  • Lead by example - Show others how to behave through your actions.
  • Think outside the box - Be creative and innovative.
  • Uphold standards - Maintain high quality and ethics.
  • Keep it professional - Avoid letting emotions or personal issues affect work.

{% call info_card('discussion', '4. Guided Discussion', initial_state='show') %}

General Workplace

  • Describe a time you saw excellent professionalism at work.
  • How can someone show professionalism without saying a word?
  • What's more important for professionalism-skills or attitude?
  • How can a team recover if someone acts unprofessionally?

Cultural Perspectives

  • Are expectations for professionalism different in your country vs. others?
  • In your culture, is "dressing for success" common or is casual clothing accepted?
  • How do people in your country usually handle disagreements at work?

Ethics & Standards

  • When is it okay to bend the rules at work? When is it not okay?
  • Is "going the extra mile" always positive, or can it be harmful?
  • Have you ever had to "keep your cool" in a stressful meeting? What happened?

Leadership & Growth

  • Do good leaders always "lead by example"?
  • How can managers encourage employees to "think outside the box"?
  • Is following everything "by the book" always the best choice?
{% endcall %}
{% call info_card('clients', '5. In-Office vs. Clients', initial_state='show') %}

A. In the Office

  • How should you greet colleagues in the morning?
  • Is it okay to share personal problems at work?
  • How do you respectfully disagree with a co-worker?
  • What is the right way to give feedback to a colleague?
  • How do you "keep it professional" when office gossip starts?

B. With Clients

  • How is your communication style different with clients compared to co-workers?
  • What are polite ways to say "no" to a client?
  • How important is first impression? What role does appearance play?
  • How should you handle a client who is upset or complaining?
  • Should you always follow rules "by the book" with clients, or sometimes bend them?

C. Comparison Questions

  • Is professionalism more important inside the office or with clients? Why?
  • Are you more relaxed with colleagues than clients? Should you be?
  • Which situations require you to "lead by example" more-in front of your team or with clients?
  • Is "thinking outside the box" more acceptable in internal meetings or client presentations? Why?
