(Teacher explains, students make their own sentences with each word.)

  • Cognitive overload - Too much mental processing, leading to fatigue or poor decisions.
  • Professional boundaries - Limits you set at work to protect your time, energy, or privacy.
  • Stealth break - A secret or hidden break taken without telling your team.
  • Normalize time off - Make rest and breaks an accepted part of company culture.
  • Emotional bandwidth - Your mental and emotional capacity to handle work or stress.
  • Self-advocate - To speak up for your own needs or well-being.
  • Strategic recovery - Planned rest that improves focus and long-term performance.

{% call info_card('reading', '3. Reading: Why Breaks Matter', initial_state='show') %}

In today's work culture, long hours and constant connectivity are often mistaken for dedication. But studies show that strategic rest is essential to avoid burnout and maintain high performance. While extended sabbaticals may be out of reach for many, well-timed short breaks can have a significant impact on mental clarity, emotional regulation, and creative thinking.

Despite the benefits, many employees feel pressure to "power through" the day, fearing judgment or appearing unproductive. These internal and external pressures lead to "stealth breaks" - moments of rest taken in secret, often tainted by guilt or anxiety, and therefore less effective.

Effective breaks, however, are intentional and restorative. Whether it's a quick walk, ten minutes of quiet, or a moment to connect with a colleague, these pauses help reset your mental state. Organizations that normalize and support healthy breaks see improved morale, resilience, and productivity across the board. But in many cases, it's up to employees to self-advocate for time off and structure their day in ways that protect their energy and well-being.

  • How can employees self-advocate for breaks in a respectful way?
