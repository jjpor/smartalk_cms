Key Terms

▾

Phrasal Verbs:

  • Own up to - To admit to something.
  • Follow through (with) - To complete an action or promise.
  • Step up - To take responsibility or initiative.
  • Carry out - To perform or complete a task.
  • Face up to - To confront or accept something.
  • Get around to - To finally do something after delaying it.
  • Pass on - To give responsibility to someone else.
  • Hold up - To delay or obstruct something.
  • Get down to - To start working on something seriously.
  • Take on - To accept responsibility or work.

Idioms:

  • Bite the bullet - To face a difficult situation bravely.
  • Come clean - To confess or admit something.
  • Throw under the bus - To betray or blame someone else.
  • Step up to the plate - To take responsibility or action.
  • Cut corners - To do something the easiest or cheapest way, often at the expense of quality.
  • Drop the ball - To make a mistake or fail to do something.
  • In the hot seat - Being in a position where you are responsible or can be criticized.
  • Pull your weight - To do your fair share of work.

{# Sezione 5: CLEAR Framework #} {% call info_card('clear-framework', 'The CLEAR Framework') %}

Use this framework to guide conversations about accountability and problem-solving.

{# Tendina interna - parte chiusa #}

A Step-by-Step Guide

▾
  • C - Clarify Expectations: Define goals, discuss roles, clarify misunderstandings.
  • L - Look Back on Commitments: Review commitments, timelines, deliverables; identify areas where expectations were not met.
  • E - Evaluate Results: Assess quality, compare outcomes to goals, analyze for improvements.
  • A - Accept Responsibility: Take ownership of outcomes (positive or negative); admit shortcomings; take proactive steps.
  • R - Resolve Any Problems: Develop an action plan, collaborate on solutions, monitor progress.
{% endcall %}
