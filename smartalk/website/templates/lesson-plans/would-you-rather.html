The Power of "Why"

This lesson uses structured hypotheticals to force choices between competing values. The goal is to articulate not just the choice, but the underlying principle driving that choice.

Focus: Justification. Can you defend your choice using a clear ethical, professional, or personal framework?

{% call info_card('phase1', '2. Phase 1: Personal & Lifestyle Lenses', initial_state='show') %}

Explore fundamental lifestyle choices and trade-offs.

Personal Preferences

  • Dinner with a movie star OR perform with your favorite musician?
  • Live in the world of the last TV show you watched OR the last book you read?
  • More free time with less money OR more money with significantly less free time?
  • Only watch pre-2000s movies OR only new releases from now on?

Travel Decisions

  • Travel back in time to meet ancestors OR future to meet descendants?
  • A year living in a van traveling OR a year in a luxury hotel in one city?
  • Flying car with limited destinations OR traditional car with unlimited international travel?

Exercise: The Consistency Check

Based on your answers, identify which value (e.g., novelty, stability, experience, legacy) appeared most consistently. Was it a conscious choice?

{% call info_card('phase3', '4. Phase 3: Societal & Future Implications', initial_state='show') %}

Debate the costs of progress and the impact of technology.

Technology & Privacy

  • Give up social media OR streaming services for a year?
  • Ultimate convenience (smart devices) with NO privacy OR total privacy with NO convenience?
  • Future dependent on tech (efficiency) OR reduced reliance (human autonomy)?

Environment & Education

  • Clean oceans yourself OR restore all rainforests with a touch?
  • All renewable but expensive energy OR cheap but polluting energy?
  • Free college for everyone OR free trade education for everyone?
  • Extremely knowledgeable on one subject OR fairly knowledgeable on many?

Exercise: Predicting the Cost

Pick one of your choices above. Now, argue against yourself: What is the single biggest unforeseen negative consequence of your choice?

{% call info_card('exercise', '5. Bonus: The Ethical Debate', initial_state='show') %}

Structured Debate Challenge

Scenario:

You chose to buy only locally produced goods (expensive) over mass-produced items (cheaper, less transparent). A colleague says your choice is elitist and only possible because you have more disposable income.

Prompt:

Construct a 3-point rebuttal. Defend the ethical framework behind your decision without sounding judgmental. Focus on systemic impact and long-term sustainability.
