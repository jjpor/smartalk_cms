{% call info_card('vocabulary', '2. Vocabulary', initial_state='show') %}

(Teacher explains, students make examples in business contexts.)

  • Disruption - Innovation that changes an industry completely.
    Example: Streaming services disrupted the movie rental industry.
  • Sustainability - Practices that protect the environment and resources.
  • Scalability - The ability of a business or idea to grow efficiently.
  • Agility - Flexibility to adapt quickly to change.
  • Adoption - When customers or businesses start using a new product or trend.
  • Early adopter - A person or company that uses new technology before most others.
  • Market shift - A major change in consumer behavior or industry conditions.
  • Innovative mindset - A way of thinking that focuses on creativity and new solutions.

Activity:

Students brainstorm which of these words best describe their company/industry today.

{% call info_card('discussion', '3. Guided Discussion', initial_state='show') %}

Trends in the Workplace

  • What trends have changed the way you work in the past 5 years?
  • Which technologies have your colleagues or company adopted recently?
  • How do trends like remote work or AI affect employee engagement?

Risks and Opportunities

  • What risks do companies face if they don't innovate?
  • Can following trends ever be dangerous or wasteful? Give examples.
  • Is it better to be an early adopter or to wait and see if a trend is successful?

Personal Experience

  • Have you ever suggested an innovative idea at work? What happened?
  • Have you ever resisted a new trend? Why?
  • Do you consider yourself innovative? Why or why not?

Future of Business

  • Which industries will be most affected by innovation in the next 10 years?
  • What's a current trend that you think will disappear soon?
  • If you could invent something for your job, what would it be?
{% endcall %}
{% call info_card('pitch', '4. Activity: Innovation Pitch', initial_state='show') %}

Instructions:

  • Students imagine they are starting a new company or improving their current company.
  • They must choose one trend (AI, sustainability, remote work, automation, customer personalization, etc.) and design a product, service, or improvement around it.
  • In pairs or small groups, they prepare a 2-minute "innovation pitch" explaining:
  • The problem.
  • The innovative idea.
  • Why it will succeed (using vocabulary like scalability, adoption, disruption).

Example:

"Our company will disrupt the restaurant industry by using AI to create personalized meal plans for customers. It's scalable because it works with any cuisine, and adoption will be fast since people already order food online."

Follow-up Discussion:

  • Which idea sounded the most innovative?
  • Which idea is the most realistic?
  • Which idea would you invest in?
