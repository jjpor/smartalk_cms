{# --- SEZIONE 4 (High/Low Context) RIMOSSA --- #} {# --- SEZIONE 5 (ora 4) RINUMERATA --- #} {% call info_card('steps', '4. Steps for Effective Cross-Cultural Communication', initial_state='show') %}
  • Step 1: Be Aware of Differences - Recognize that your way is not the only way.
  • Step 2: Listen and Observe - Notice body language, tone, and timing.
  • Step 3: Clarify and Confirm - Ask polite questions to avoid miscommunication.
  • Step 4: Adapt Your Style - Adjust formality, directness, or tone depending on the culture.
  • Step 5: Find Common Ground - Focus on shared goals rather than differences.
  • Step 6: Reflect and Learn - Think about what worked, what didn't, and apply it next time.

Activity: Students apply these steps to one of the scenarios (below).

{% endcall %}
{# --- SEZIONE 6 (ora 5) RINUMERATA --- #} {% call info_card('scenarios', '5. Scenarios (Speaking Practice)', initial_state='show') %}
  • You're working with a colleague from a culture where saying "no" directly is impolite. How do you understand what they mean?
  • You're giving feedback to a team member from a more formal culture. How should you adjust your style?
  • You are in a meeting with people from high-context and low-context cultures. How do you make sure everyone understands?
  • Your international client prefers long relationship-building conversations before doing business. How do you handle it if you prefer directness?
  • A global team is confused because some people arrive late to meetings (cultural differences in time). How do you resolve it?
