Tips & Techniques

In this section, you will find the fundamental strategies to prepare for a salary negotiation, feel valued in your role, and get what you deserve.

Research is Key

Understand the market value for your role in your industry and location. Websites like Glassdoor, Payscale, or industry reports can provide valuable insights. Be ready to explain why you believe you deserve a higher salary.

Timing is Everything

Ideally, negotiate after you've received a job offer but before you've accepted it... If you're already employed, choose an appropriate time...

Practice Your Pitch

Clearly communicate your value to the organization. Highlight your accomplishments... Practice your talking points...

Be Confident and Positive

Approach the negotiation with confidence, but remain positive and respectful...

Consider the Whole Package

Don't just focus on the base salary. Consider other benefits such as bonuses, stock options...

Understand Your Priorities

Identify your "non-negotiables" and the areas where you might be more flexible...

Express gratitude and enthusiasm

▾ {# Usiamo la freccia standard #}
  • "I appreciate the opportunities I've had here and I am excited about the future."

Presenting your case

▾
  • "Reflecting on my contributions, I have accomplished [mention successes] and have taken on additional responsibilities."

Research-based approach

▾
  • "I've done some market research, and the industry standard for someone in my role and with my experience is [mention a specific figure]."

Highlighting skills and value

▾
  • "I believe my skills in [mention specific skills] have contributed significantly to the success of the team."

Asking for a salary review

▾
  • "Considering my achievements and market standards, I would like to discuss the possibility of a salary review."
