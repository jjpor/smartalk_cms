{% endcall %} {# Sezione 2: Vocabulary #} {% call info_card('vocabulary', 'Vocabulary Introduction') %}

Review these key terms and their definitions related to teamwork.

Key Terms

▾
  • Teamwork: The combined action of a group, especially when effective and efficient.
  • Collaboration: The action of working with someone to produce or create something.
  • Synergy: The interaction of two or more agents to produce a combined effect greater than the sum of their separate effects.
  • Consensus: General agreement among a group.
  • Conflict Resolution: A way to find a peaceful solution to a disagreement.
  • Cohesion: The action or fact of forming a united whole.
  • Delegation: The assignment of responsibility or authority to another person.
  • Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of another.
{% endcall %} {# Sezione 3: Team Roles #} {% call info_card('team-roles', 'Team Roles') %}

Teams are made up of more than just job titles. People often take on informal roles that contribute to the group's dynamic.

Informal Roles in Teams

▾
  • The Peacemaker - Calms tension and smooths over conflict to keep harmony in the group.
  • The Skeptic - Questions ideas, points out risks, and makes the group think more critically.
  • The Energizer - Motivates the group, brings enthusiasm, and keeps energy levels high.
  • The Quiet Worker - Stays in the background, avoids attention, but gets tasks done reliably.
  • The Leader (Unofficial) - Takes charge informally, influences direction, and organizes others without an official title.
  • The Bridge-Builder - Connects people across departments or groups, making sure communication flows.
  • The Detail Keeper - Notices small mistakes, tracks details, and follows up on loose ends.
  • The Joker - Lightens the mood with humor, helps reduce stress, and makes teamwork more enjoyable.
  • The Challenger - Pushes the team to aim higher, challenge limits, and think beyond the obvious.

Conversation Topics:

  • Which of these roles do you naturally play in a team?
  • Which role do you think is the most valuable in business? Why?
  • Have you ever worked with a strong “Challenger” or “Skeptic”? How did it affect the outcome?
  • Do you think a “Joker” helps or distracts in professional teamwork?
  • Can one person play multiple roles depending on the situation?
  • Which role do you find most challenging to work with? Why?
  • Do teams need a balance of these roles to succeed, or can one dominate?
