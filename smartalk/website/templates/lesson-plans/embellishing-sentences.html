To embellish a sentence is to make it sound more engaging, softer, more natural, or more persuasive. It moves you from simply conveying data to communicating with tone.

Tools for Embellishment:

  • Adverbs: “honestly”, “actually”, “surprisingly”, “frankly”.
  • Contextual Clauses: “you know what I mean”, “at the end of the day”, “from my point of view”.
  • Tone Softeners/Intensifiers: “kind of”, “a bit”, “really”, “quite”.
  • Purpose Phrases: “just to be clear”, “what I mean is”, “the point is”.
  • Mini Stories/Examples: “like, last week it happened to me...”.

{% call info_card('business-prompts', '3. Base Sentences (Business)', initial_state='show') %}

Focus on using softeners like "might," "could," "a bit," and "from my perspective."

  • We should rethink this strategy.
  • I’m not convinced this will work.
  • The results are not satisfying.
  • We need to clarify our priorities.
  • I don’t think that’s sustainable.
  • Let’s take a step back and review.
  • We might need to involve the client.
  • That’s not aligned with our values.
  • We could handle this differently.
  • I’d rather wait before making a decision.
  • I’d prefer a more structured approach.
  • I’m concerned about the timing.
  • The communication could be clearer.
  • That’s not what I expected.
  • Let’s make sure we’re on the same page.
