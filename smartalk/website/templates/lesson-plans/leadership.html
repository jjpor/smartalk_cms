These questions are designed to help you reflect on your leadership style.

{# Usiamo la classe master .lesson-card #}

Your Leadership Style

▾ {# Usiamo la freccia standard #}
  • How would you describe your leadership style?
  • What leadership qualities do you think are most important in your role?
  • How do you adapt your leadership style for different team members or situations?
  • Can you share an example where you had to change your leadership approach to better suit your team?
  • How do you handle conflicts within your team as a leader?

Reflecting on Good Leadership

▾
  • Who is a leader you admire and why?
  • Can you describe a situation where a leader you admire had a significant impact on your career?
  • What leadership traits did they show that you try to emulate in your own leadership?
  • How did their leadership style influence the culture of the organization?
  • What have you learned from a great leader that you still apply today?

Leadership Challenges and Growth

▾
  • What is the most challenging aspect of being a leader for you?
  • How do you measure your effectiveness as a leader?
  • How do you foster leadership qualities in others within your team?
  • Can you discuss a time you struggled as a leader and what you learned from that experience?
  • What steps do you take to continuously improve your leadership skills?

Leadership in Practice

▾
  • How do you balance the need for results with maintaining team morale?
  • What strategies do you use to motivate and inspire your team?
  • How do you approach the decision-making process as a leader?
  • How do you handle failure within your team or department?
  • What role does communication play in your leadership style, and how do you ensure it is effective?

Learn about some of the most common leadership styles.

Common Styles

▾
  • Autocratic: The leader makes decisions without consulting team members.
  • Democratic: The leader includes team members in the decision-making process.
  • Laissez-Faire: The leader provides minimal guidance...
  • Transformational: The leader inspires and motivates...
  • Transactional: The leader focuses on daily operations...
  • Servant: The leader prioritizes the well-being...
  • Charismatic: The leader has a magnetic personality...
  • Coaching: The leader focuses on developing skills...
  • Visionary: The leader has a clear vision...
  • Situational: The leader adapts their style...

Key Styles Summary

▾
  • Autocratic Leadership: ...expects compliance without input...
  • Democratic (Participative) Leadership: ...involves team members... fostering collaboration...
  • Transformational Leadership: ...inspires and motivates... achieve exceptional outcomes...
  • Transactional Leadership: ...emphasizes structure, clear tasks, and rewards/punishments...
  • Laissez-Faire Leadership: ...minimal guidance, allowing team members to make decisions...
  • Servant Leadership: ...prioritizes the team's needs, focusing on empowering...
  • Situational Leadership: ...adapts their style based on maturity, competence, and situation...

Practice Scenarios
