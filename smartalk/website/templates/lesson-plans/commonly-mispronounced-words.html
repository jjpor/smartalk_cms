English pronunciation can be tricky because words are not always spelled the way they sound. Today we'll practice groups of words that learners often mispronounce, focusing on patterns, stress, and tricky sounds.

{% call info_card('patterns', '2. Common Patterns', initial_state='show') %}

-ous

The "ous" rhymes with miss (not "us" or "mouse").

  • various, serious, nervous, obvious, previous

-tion / -sion

Pronounced /ʃən/ (like shin).

  • information, section, decision, population, association, organization, position, commission

-ate

Verbs rhyme with eight. Nouns and adjectives rhyme with it.

  • private, separate, demonstrate, concentrate, corporate, initiate, negotiate, adequate

-ence / -ance

End with the sound /əns/.

  • violence, dependence, experience, conference, evidence, resistance, performance, importance

/dʒ/ sound (like German)

  • educate / education, graduate / graduation, soldier, individual, procedure

ch = k

The "ch" sounds like k.

  • chemical, mechanical, psychiatry, ache, technique, chaos

Noun vs. Verb Stress

Nouns → stress on the first syllable. Verbs → stress on the second syllable.

Self / Selves Stress

Stress goes on the second syllable.

  • myself, yourself, himself, herself, ourselves, themselves

Numbers – teen vs. ty

Teen → stress on the second syllable (sixTEEN). Ty → stress on the first syllable (SIXty).

  • fourteen / forty
  • fifteen / fifty
  • sixteen / sixty
  • seventeen / seventy

Weird Stress Words

Words often stressed incorrectly by learners:

  • hotel, engineer, museum, comfortable, recipe, cousin, iron, lawyer, desert vs. dessert, dangerous, popular, mayor / major, rural, scissors, status, surface, European

{% call info_card('other-tricks', '4. Other Tricky Sounds', initial_state='show') %}

Silent letters

Each of these words contains at least one silent letter.

  • receipt, answer, vegetable, towards, camera, salmon, basically / physically / typically, February, laboratory, temperature, corporate, average, difference, often, Wednesday, whistle, castle

French words

The "t" is silent.

  • ballet, valet, buffet, merlot, chalet

Glottal Stop

We often don't pronounce the "t," instead cutting it off in the throat.

  • button, mountain, certain, curtain, fountain, important

t → d (flap sound in American English)

The "t" often sounds like a soft "d."

  • better, butter, turtle, later, literature, bottle, computer

/ʊ/ sound (like "good")

  • put, could, should, would, wood, took
