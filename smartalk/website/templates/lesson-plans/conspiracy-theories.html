What are Conspiracy Theories?', initial_state='show') %}

A conspiracy theory is a belief that a secret group of people is controlling or manipulating events for their own benefit, often in a way that the general public is unaware of.

Key Characteristics

  • ✅ A Secret Plan – A powerful group working in shadows.
  • ✅ Manipulation – Events are thought to be staged or covered up.
  • ✅ Lack of Solid Evidence – Reliance on speculation or coincidence.
  • ✅ "Us vs. Them" – Believers think they have "insider knowledge".
{% endcall %}
{% call info_card('grammar', '2. Grammar: Hedging Language', initial_state='show') %}

Hedging language is a way of making statements less direct, more cautious, or polite. It adds "softeners" to avoid absolute claims.

Useful when you are unsure, discussing sensitive topics, or writing professionally.

How to use it

Modal Verbs

might, could, may

"The government might have hidden it."

Cautious Adverbs

possibly, probably, generally

"This theory is possibly true."

Passive/Impersonal

It is claimed that...

"It is believed that aliens built it."

Distancing Phrases

Many people believe...

"Some argue that the earth is flat."
