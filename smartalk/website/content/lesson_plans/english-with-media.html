1. Lesson Goal: Active Viewing

The Difference Between Watching & Learning

▾

This plan is designed to turn your screen time into a structured language lesson. Simply watching English media is passive; this process ensures you actively engage with pronunciation, vocabulary, and natural conversational flow.

The focus is on the repetition cycle: Listen > Read > Repeat > Use.

2. Phase 1: Preparation & Input

Choosing the right resource and maximizing comprehension during your first two watches.

Content Selection & Subtitle Strategy (Steps 1 & 2)

▾
1. Choose the Right Content
  • Pick a show or movie that interests you and matches your level.
  • Start with family-friendly shows, sitcoms, or dramas with clear dialogue.
2. Use Subtitles Wisely
  1. First Watch: Use English subtitles to understand the context.
  2. Second Watch: Turn off subtitles and focus on listening.

Question: Content Level Check

Name one English show you currently enjoy. Is it too challenging or just right? Why?
