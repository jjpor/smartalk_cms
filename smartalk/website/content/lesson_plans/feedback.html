A Guide to Giving and Receiving Feedback

Mastering the art of effective feedback for personal and professional growth.

  • Receiving Feedback

Giving Feedback

The Feedback Decalogue

▾
  • Be Specific and Timely: Give clear examples as promptly as possible.
  • Focus on Behavior, Not Personality: Address specific actions, not character.
  • Balance Positivity and Constructiveness: Highlight strengths and areas for growth.

Scenarios for Practice

▾
  1. Missed Deadline: An employee consistently misses deadlines.
    • How would you approach the conversation to be supportive?
    • What action plan could you create together?
  2. Poor Quality Work: A team member produces low-quality work.
    • How can you give feedback without discouraging them?
    • What examples would you use to define "quality"?

Receiving Feedback

The Receiving Feedback Decalogue

▾
  • Listen Attentively: Pay close attention without interrupting.
  • Remain Calm: Stay open-minded, even if it's difficult.
  • Seek Clarity: Ask for specific examples.
