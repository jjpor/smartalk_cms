1. Introduction & Overview

Objectives and Structure

▾

The goal of this lesson is to familiarize you with the structure, format, and scoring of the TOEFL iBT exam. We will cover the types of tasks in each section and understand the requirements for achieving a target B2 level performance.

Exam Structure:

  • Reading: Comprehension of academic texts.
  • Listening: Understanding lectures and conversations.
  • Speaking: Expressing opinions and integrating information.
  • Writing: Composing integrated and independent essays.

2. Reading Section

Duration: Typically lasts about 60–80 minutes.

Academic Texts and Question Formats

▾

Topics & Skills

Passages cover a variety of academic subjects such as history, social science, natural science, and literature. The test assesses skills like: reading comprehension, identifying main ideas, understanding details, making inferences, and understanding vocabulary in context.

Question Types

  • Multiple-Choice: Choose the best answer from several options.
  • Insert Text: Select the option that fits best in a blank space within the passage.

Self-Assessment Reading Task (Adapted Sample)

Critical thinking is a vital skill that enables individuals to analyze information, evaluate arguments, and make reasoned decisions. In today's fast-paced world... employers seek individuals who can analyze complex problems, think independently, and propose effective solutions.

Questions:

  1. What is critical thinking, and why is it important in today's world?
  2. Why are critical thinking skills valued in professional settings?
