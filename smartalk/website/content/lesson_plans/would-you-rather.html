1. Lesson Goal

The Power of "Why"

▾

This lesson uses structured hypotheticals to force choices between competing values (e.g., money vs. ethics, privacy vs. convenience, personal gain vs. team success). The goal is to articulate not just the choice, but the underlying principle driving that choice.

Focus: Justification. Can you defend your choice using a clear ethical, professional, or personal framework?

2. Phase 1: Personal & Lifestyle Lenses

Goal: Explore fundamental lifestyle choices, trade-offs in leisure, and the balance between past and future.

Pop Culture, Leisure, and Travel

▾

Personal Preferences

  • 1. Would you rather attend a private dinner with your favorite movie star or perform on stage with your favorite musician?
  • 2. Would you rather live in the world of the last TV show you watched or the last book you read?

Exercise: The Consistency Check

Based on your answers, identify which value (e.g., novelty, stability, experience, legacy) appeared most consistently.
