Introduction

Feedback is a powerful tool for growth, but it's not always easy to give or receive. This guide will help you navigate workplace feedback by providing a framework, key vocabulary, and practical exercises to turn vague comments into clear, actionable steps.

Questions to Consider

▾
  • When was the last time you received feedback at work? What was it about?
  • Do you usually feel that feedback at your job is helpful? Why or why not?
  • What makes feedback difficult to give or receive?
  • In your language, is feedback typically direct or indirect? How is that different from English-speaking workplaces?

Key Vocabulary

Understanding the language of feedback is the first step toward mastering it. Let's clarify a few key terms.

Clear vs. Ambiguous

▾
  • Can you give an example of ambiguous vs. clear feedback?
  • Example: "Be more strategic" (Ambiguous) vs. "Next time, include market trends and competitor analysis in your report to show a strategic perspective" (Clear).

Self-Awareness

▾
  • Why is self-awareness important when receiving feedback?
  • Self-awareness allows you to understand your own strengths and weaknesses, so you can receive feedback without defensiveness.
