Introduction & Explanation

The verbs make and do can be tricky. While both describe an action, they are used in different contexts. Understanding the subtle differences will help you sound more natural and precise.

We use 'MAKE' when we talk about...

Key Uses of 'MAKE'

▾
  • Creating or producing something: (e.g., She made a cake.)
  • Producing a reaction or effect: (e.g., This movie makes me happy.)
  • Plans and decisions: (e.g., make a decision, make a choice)
  • Food, drink, and meals: (e.g., make breakfast, make coffee)
  • Speaking and sounds: (e.g., make a comment, make a noise)

Tip: Think about creating, producing, or causing something new.

We use 'DO' when we talk about...

Key Uses of 'DO'

▾
  • Work, jobs, and tasks: (e.g., do your homework, do a good job)
  • General/unspecified activities: (e.g., do something, do nothing)
  • Replacing verbs when the action is clear: (e.g., do the dishes, do your hair)
  • Important expressions: (e.g., do well, do business, do a favor)

Tip: Think about actions, tasks, and responsibilities.
